<div dir="ltr" style="text-align:left;"><div class="separator" style="clear:both;text-align:center;"><a href="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/88e35-inder_davalur_blogs_datahype.png" style="margin-left:1em;margin-right:1em;"><img border="0" height="360" src="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/21890-inder_davalur_blogs_datahype.png" width="640" /></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear:both;text-align:center;"></div><span>DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION AND THE PLACE FOR DATA</span><br /><div dir="ltr" id="docs-internal-guid-2a068dbc-fe6e-999c-4605-22a11f0a3f6b" style="line-height:2.0736;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:6pt;text-align:justify;"><hr /></div><div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom:0;margin-top:6pt;text-align:justify;"><div style="line-height:2.0736;"><div dir="ltr" id="docs-internal-guid-7f8576b5-ff00-784f-b097-8486d6015797" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Mea Culpa, I am one of those who is guilty of getting on and staying on </span></span><span>the Big Data wagon for the wrong reasons. “Data is the new oil” is an </span><span>oft-repeated phrase. I am about to commit a “virtual” suicide by </span><span>proclaiming that it is not so. Data has its place and it is not at the top of </span><span>the digital food chain. I feel that we have crowned the half-naked </span><span>prince, Emperor in haste.</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">For the sake of clarity, when I say data, I will be referring to digital data throughout this piece. Data is a </span></span><span>by-product of any activity. Therefore, creating data is as natural as breathing. So we have data. A lot of </span><span>data. So what? Accumulating data, structuring it, storing it, analyzing it are a natural progression from </span><span>that point onwards. How and what we do with the data is more important. Software. </span><br /><span><br /></span> <span>The magic that is software, to me, is more transfixing. Consider the prospect of a language written in a </span><span>semantic that is alien to our natural human language. A cryptic command, logic, condition, trigger – </span><span>anything at all – that is magically read, understood and acted upon by silicon. Hardware that contains </span><span>baked-in code that can parse and carry out complex instructions at blazing speeds. Pieces of such chips </span><span>soldered on a board and communicating through ‘roadways’ of circuits laid out on a board. The miracle </span><span>of hardware coupled with the magic that is software is what gets my adrenalin pumping. How can such </span><span>a marvel not be exciting?</span><br /><span><br /></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Even the awesomeness of hardware pales in comparison to software. Hardware is more or less static. It </span></span><span>is confined to physical and functional dimensions. Software, however, is supreme. It can use the same </span><span>hardware (with some limitations of course) and carry out simple tasks, entertain with games, or </span><span>perform wildly complex calculations at very very high rates of speed, accurately all the time. And it can </span><span>do this million million times with alacrity. This is just the beginning of what software can do. But wait, </span><span>there’s more!</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Consider intelligence in software. It suddenly becomes a living, breathing, dynamic being. Almost. </span></span><span>Software can learn and teach itself. Crunching data and spitting out patterns and actionable analysis </span><span>suddenly becomes mundane, banal almost pedestrian. No. I am not against data or big data. By itself, </span><span>big data is just that. A monstrosity. Sometimes, big data actually gets in the way. Misleads us in making </span><span>decisions quickly. Software breathes life into data. </span><br /><span><br /></span> <span>Take any software language or tool. Examine it. Study its flow, the eloquence, the nuance and its </span><span>brilliance. Brevity in software coding is revered by programming perfectionists. There is elegance in a </span><span>well-written piece of code that executes beautifully, perfectly, every time. Anyone that can find literary </span><span>melody in Shakespeare or Milton can certainly begin to enjoy the harmony in a beautifully crafted </span><span>software application code. So, my appeal goes out to all those who are worshipping big data to take a </span><span>moment to reflect upon the joy that software brings to our daily lives.

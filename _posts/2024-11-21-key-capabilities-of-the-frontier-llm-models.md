<p>Here are some of the key capabilities of the frontier models discussed in the sources:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">GPT-4</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Multimodal Model:</strong> GPT-4 is a large multimodal model, meaning it can process and understand various input types, including text and images. It exhibits human-level performance on select professional and academic benchmarks.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Predictive Text Generation:</strong> As a Transformer-style model, GPT-4 is fundamentally trained to predict the next token in a sequence. It leverages publicly available data, such as internet content, and licensed data from third-party providers for training.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF):</strong> GPT-4's training incorporates RLHF to fine-tune its responses and align them with human preferences. This process enhances the model's ability to provide helpful and coherent outputs.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Analytical Writing:</strong> GPT-4 can generate high-quality essays in response to prompts, achieving top scores on standardized writing assessments.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Code Explanation and Vulnerability Analysis:</strong> GPT-4 can explain code snippets, particularly vulnerabilities, if the code fits within its context window. However, its ability to create exploits for identified vulnerabilities is limited.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Gemini Family</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Multimodal Capabilities:</strong> Gemini models are inherently multimodal, proficiently handling and integrating information across different modalities. They excel at extracting information from visuals like charts, tables, and figures, combining this with advanced reasoning skills. Examples of this include generating matplotlib code based on visual input and rearranging subplots.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>State-of-the-Art Performance:</strong> The Gemini family, especially Gemini Ultra, demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on diverse benchmarks for tasks spanning language, coding, reasoning, and multimodal understanding. It outperforms other models in areas like image understanding, mathematical reasoning, and multilingual tasks.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Reasoning and STEM Proficiency:</strong> Gemini Ultra exhibits remarkable reasoning abilities and strong competence in STEM subjects. It excels on benchmarks like MMLU, exceeding human expert performance and showcasing its knowledge and problem-solving skills across various domains. This positions it as a potential tool for educational applications, such as personalized learning and intelligent tutoring.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Factuality Focus:</strong> Factuality is a major emphasis in the training and deployment of Gemini models. They are designed to minimize the generation of incorrect or hallucinated information, especially in fact-seeking prompts.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>On-Device Deployment:</strong> The Gemini family includes Gemini Nano, a series of models specifically designed for on-device use. These models are optimized for efficiency and size, making them suitable for tasks like summarization, reading comprehension, and text completion on devices with limited resources.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Claude 3 Family</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Multimodal Input:</strong> Claude 3 models are multimodal, accepting both text and image inputs. This enables them to process and analyze visual data, expanding their capabilities to a wider range of use cases.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Vision Capabilities:</strong> Claude 3 possesses visual understanding capabilities, allowing it to process images and extract information from them. It can convert handwritten text in images to digital text and organize it into formats like JSON. Additionally, it can recognize objects and understand their mathematical properties.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Advanced Reasoning, Math, and Coding:</strong> Claude 3 demonstrates strong performance in reasoning, math, and coding tasks. Its reasoning capabilities are highlighted in its success on benchmarks like GPQA, MMLU, and MMMU. It excels in solving math problems in both English and multilingual settings. Claude 3 also shows proficiency in code generation and understanding.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Long Context Handling:</strong> Claude 3 models are capable of handling long contexts effectively. They perform well in tasks that involve question-answering and information retrieval from extended texts.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Tool Use:</strong> Claude 3 exhibits proficiency in tool use or function calling. This allows it to be seamlessly integrated into specialized applications and custom workflows, enhancing its practicality in diverse real-world scenarios.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Llama 3 Family</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Multilingual Support:</strong> Llama 3 is inherently multilingual, supporting a wide range of languages for various tasks.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Coding, Reasoning, and Tool Usage:</strong> Llama 3 models are proficient in coding, demonstrating strong reasoning abilities, and effectively utilizing tools. This makes them suitable for complex tasks that require code generation, problem-solving, and interaction with external tools.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Long Context Handling:</strong> Llama 3 excels in long context understanding, as evidenced by its performance on benchmarks like ZeroSCROLLS, Needle-in-a-Haystack, and InfiniteBench. It can process and retain information from extended texts, enabling it to answer questions, summarize content, and extract relevant details accurately.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Safety and Responsibility:</strong> The development of Llama 3 prioritizes safety and responsible AI principles. Extensive efforts are made to mitigate risks through benchmark construction, red teaming, and the development of system-level safety mechanisms. This includes building classifiers for input and output filtering to prevent misuse and promote ethical AI deployment.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>

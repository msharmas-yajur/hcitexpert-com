The article is republished here with the authors' permission</i></span></span></div><div style="margin:0;"><br /></div><div style="margin:0;"><span>24 Mar 2016,<span class="Apple-converted-space"> India</span></span></div><span>─</span><br /><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><br /></span></b><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;">Abstract</span></b><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"> </b><br /><div class="article-body" dir="ltr" style="background:transparent;border:0;box-sizing:border-box;color:rgba(0,0,0,0.701961);font-family:georgia, serif;font-stretch:inherit;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;font-weight:normal;letter-spacing:normal;line-height:inherit;margin:0;outline:0;overflow:hidden;padding:0;text-indent:0;text-transform:none;vertical-align:baseline;white-space:normal;word-spacing:0;"><div style="background-attachment:scroll;background-clip:initial;background-color:transparent;background-image:none;background-origin:initial;background-position:0 0;background-repeat:repeat;background-size:initial;border-image-outset:initial;border-image-repeat:initial;border-image-slice:initial;border-image-source:initial;border-image-width:initial;border:0 none;box-sizing:border-box;color:#232629;font-family:inherit;font-stretch:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;font-weight:200;line-height:32px;margin:0 0 32px;outline:none 0;padding:0;text-align:justify;vertical-align:baseline;"><div style="font-size:18px;">The lucrativeness offered by the healthcare IT industry has brought significant attention worldwide. Healthcare industry is considered ever green and healthcare IT has attracted the attention of global players for investments. There are a section of players who are pioneers in the industry and have recorded significant growth. At the same time there are laggards who want to build such capabilities and tap the untapped market following the recent industry trends and success stories. Healthcare IT organisations have to cope with transforming business model while adhering to strict regulatory demands of the industry. They need to carefully adopt strategies based on the organisational maturity, capital and the time they have for go-to-market. In pursuit of quick success, often organisations commit mistakes choosing the right approach while building potential health IT product capabilities. This article highlights different strategic options for Indian IT product-centered organisations thinking of foraying into the healthcare industry</div><div style="font-size:18px;"><br /><a name='more'></a><br /></div><div style="font-size:18px;"><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;">Global Healthcare and Healthcare IT spending trends</span></b></span></b><br /><br /></div><div style="font-size:18px;"><span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;">According to industry estimates, the global healthcare spending is  expected to grow at an average of 5.2 per cent year on year during 2014 –  2018 to a total of $9.3 trillion. In spite of its vastness, both  developed and emerging nations are dealing with issues like ageing  population, rising incidences of chronic diseases, rapidly increasing  cost of healthcare, disparity in quality of care, infrastructural  challenges, workforce shortage, non-uniform distribution of healthcare  facilities across the community locations etc. The burden faced globally  today has never been so challenging than ever it was.</span></div><div style="font-size:18px;"><span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><br /></span> An estimate by the Economist Intelligence Unit (EIU) the regional  healthcare spending during the year 2015 is expected to be as given in  the table.</div><div style="font-size:18px;"><br /></div><div style="font-size:18px;"><a href="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/de023-spendingpergdp.gif" style="clear:right;float:right;margin-bottom:1em;margin-left:1em;"><img border="0" src="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/de023-spendingpergdp.gif" /></a>Globally, most nations have been challenged to improve quality of  care while <span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;">reducing the cost of healthcare by means of inventing cost  effective methods for an optimal outcome. The challenges described above  have led to adoption of healthcare IT solutions such as Electronic  Medical Records (EMR)/ Electronic Health Records (EHR) for safe storage  of healthcare information and to make more informed decision.</span></div><div style="font-size:18px;"><br /></div><div style="font-size:18px;">According to an estimate by MarketsandMarkets, a leading research  organisation, the global healthcare IT market is estimated to reach  $56.7 billion by 2017 — up from $40.4 billion in 2012 — due to the  demand for clinical information technology, administrative solutions and  services. Among the various healthcare IT solutions offered, EMR/ EHR  segment dominates the sector.</div><div style="font-size:18px;"><br /></div><div style="font-size:18px;">Gartner estimates healthcare providers in India are likely to spend  $1.2 billion on healthcare IT products and services in 2015, a seven per  cent growth over 2014. Software spending is likely to grow 6.2 per cent  to reach $103 million in 2015, up from $97 million in 2014, led by  growth in vertical specific software. An estimate by Frost &amp;  Sullivan, healthcare information technology market in India is expected  to reach $1.45 billion in 2018 mainly due to fast adoption of technology  by stake-holders. India being a developing nation, the health IT  spending is still much less than that of the developed nations. <span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;">The rest of the paper discusses various drivers for healthcare IT  initiatives, its attractiveness and approaches for Indian health IT  organisations for an effective go-to-market strategy.</span></div></div><div style="background-attachment:scroll;background-clip:initial;background-color:transparent;background-image:none;background-origin:initial;background-position:0 style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;">Drivers of Healthcare IT in India</span></b><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"> </b></span></b><br /><br /><span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;">Some of the major factors contributing to the growth of healthcare IT globally are listed below:</span><br /><ul><li>The continuously growing pressure to cut healthcare costs</li><li>Need for care coordination and management demanding integrated healthcare systems</li><li>High rate of return on investment in healthcare systems</li><li>Financial support and incentives from the government</li><li>Growth of medical tourism</li><li>Government initiatives, conducive policies for the sector</li><li>The rise in the ageing population</li><li>Growing demand of health IT products to reduce medication errors</li><li>Rise in incidences of chronic and lifestyle disorders</li><li>More informed and engaged patients.</li></ul><div><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><br /></span></b><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;">Why do more and more Indian IT organisations want to venture into Healthcare Industry? </span></b><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"> </b></span></b></span></b><br /><span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;">Healthcare has a huge addressable market. India as a country has  witnessed a rather steep growth trajectory only in the last decade with  the advent of the private sector. The influence of technology has been  an important growth driver, with healthcare models based on IT  intervention now becoming a reality. The accessibility of healthcare  today is more than it ever was, consequently increasing the opportunity  for new players. Emergence of new delivery models which are scalable,  less capital intensive and yet promise better earnings is one of the  major reasons the healthcare sector has lured the investors. Diagnostic  chains, single speciality clinics, wellness centres, primary care  set-ups etc. are all emerging models of healthcare and it is still  evolving. In a nutshell, healthcare industry provides array of  opportunities for new experiments at a promising return on investments.  The penetration of healthcare IT is still in its infancy providing ample  opportunities to all competent healthcare IT solution providers. With  increased importance to healthcare needs, the adoption of modern  healthcare IT systems is bound to grow.</span></div><div><span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><br /></span><br /><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;">Approaches for Go-to-Market </span></b><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"> </b></span></b></span></b></div><div><span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><br /></span><span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;">In order to tap the business opportunity presented by the IT  enablement of healthcare institutions, the IT organisation should  evaluate options and consider one that suits the best. A single approach  may not fit all. In pursuit of quick success, often IT organisations  opt for suboptimal options, which may not meet their long term  objectives. Following are the three strategic options to realise the  business opportunity presented by healthcare IT market:</span></div><div><ul><li><b>Approach 1:</b> MODIFY Enhancing an open source software</li><li><b>Approach 2:</b> CREATE Building a greenfield system</li><li><b>Approach 3:</b> ACQUIRE Acquiring a licensed product</li></ul>The table below provides the advantages and disadvantages associated with each of the approaches.<br />An organisation should evaluate the functional, technical and  business capabilities while prioritising a strategic approach. Each of  the approaches can be evaluated based on the following parameters:<br /><br /><b><u>Cost and effort</u></b><br /><b>Total cost of ownership (TCO):</b> Total cost of  ownership refers to the cost to the organisation for sustaining a  product line. This includes the license fee for the product and cost of  the application maintenance and support.<br /><b>Effort:</b> This is the effort that needs to be put in  by the organisation to meet the desired product criteria driven by  Customer/ Market needs.<br /><b>Potential Revenue:</b> This refers to the revenue that would be generated by the organisation on entering the market with the stated product.<br /><b><br /></b> <b><u>Market factors</u></b><br /><b>Time to market:</b> This is the time taken to launch the product in the market for customers.<br /><b>Market acceptability risk:</b> The risk that the product  launched into the market is not accepted by the clients. This is  particularly high for a new product launch.<br /><b><br /></b> <b><u>System related factors</u></b><br /><b>Customisability:</b> The capability of a system to be  easily customised for desired features. Creation of a greenfield system  offers the highest amount of flexibility as it can be designed keeping  product expansion in mind.<br /><b>Scalability:</b> The capability of a system to be easily  scalable for larger implementations. While a greenfield system can be  designed to be scalable an open source or acquired product may have  limitations.<br /><b>Skilled resource availability:</b> The major constraint  of a system is the technology stack that it is built on. Having  technically sound resources trained exclusively on the same platform is  one of the major factors for system selection.<br /><b>Legal/ IP Risk:</b> Enhancing and commercialising  existing open source systems would give rise to potential legal risks.  Some systems are covered under various public licenses which prohibit  the commercial use for profit.<br /><b>Security risk:</b> With the ever growing population that  is being catered through the IT enabled system, security of the  healthcare data plays a vital role in the evaluation of a system. Many  laws of the land mandate patient privacy and prohibit the transfer or  usage of the healthcare data and thus require utmost authorisation and  protection for the same. Any shortcomings in the system that compromises  patient data security would pose a security risk.<br />The evaluation of each of the approaches based on the above mentioned parameters are summarised in Figure 2.<br /><br /><div class="separator" style="clear:both;text-align:center;"><a href="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/e4407-evaluationparamsfig2.gif" style="margin-left:1em;margin-right:1em;"><img border="0" height="212" src="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/7895a-evaluationparamsfig2.gif" width="400" /></a></div><br /><br /><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;">Key Considerations </span></b><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"> </b></span></b></span></b><br /><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><br /></b></span></b></span></b><span style="font-family:inherit;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;">A greenfield system though requires investments, is a safer approach  as it minimises some the risks demonstrated in other two approaches.  However, all organisations may not be in a position to have proprietary  systems to exploit the immediate market opportunities presented. The  pioneers always have the advantage to exploit the market opportunities  with less competition. New players should carefully choose the market  segment they want to make an entry. When an organisation lacks  significant market presence and experience, it may be recommended that  the organisation should take baby steps and stay profitable yet. A big  bang approach may not support the ultimate objectives of the  organisation. In this context it may be recommended to health IT vendors  that the</span><br />Approach –‘MODIFY’ can be considered as a short term strategy  with an objective of learnings, small scale implementations and R&amp;D.<br />Approaches – ‘CREATE’ and ‘ACQUIRE’ can be considered as long term  strategies with an objective of achieving the vision of the  organisation.<br />Approach – CREATE demands investment in terms of time and  ensuring the product exceeds expectations than a competing product while  <br />Approach – ACQUIRE enables the organisation a quicker go-to-market for realising the opportunities. However, the risks associated with the approach must be given due consideration.<br /><br /><div class="separator" style="clear:both;text-align:center;"><a href="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/cab5b-prosconsofapproaches.gif" style="margin-left:1em;margin-right:1em;"><img border="0" height="361" src="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/4a29b-prosconsofapproaches.gif" width="640" /></a></div><br /><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;">Conclusions </span></b><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"> </b></span></b></span></b><br /><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><br /></b></span></b></span></b>The lucrativeness of the healthcare IT market has received  significant attention. It is an obvious choice to exploit the  opportunities presented. The pioneers and the laggards are equally keen  to make the best of the opportunities. An approach that works best for a  pioneer may not be of same value to a beginner. In pursuit of quick  success, organisations should not commit the mistakes of engaging in a  wrong approach to make a foray in hurry. It has been rightly said,  “There is no shortcut to success.” Organisations need to carry out a  risk and return tradeoff before formulating and executing any strategies  for the best possible outcome.<br /><br /><br /><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;">References </span></b><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"> </b></span></b></span></b><br /><b style="color:#695d46;font-family:'pt sans narrow';font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;line-height:normal;text-align:left;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:14px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><span style="font-size:large;"><b style="font-size:18.6667px;font-style:inherit;font-variant:inherit;"><br /></b></span></b></span></b><i><b>i.</b> 2015 Global Healthcare Outlook.

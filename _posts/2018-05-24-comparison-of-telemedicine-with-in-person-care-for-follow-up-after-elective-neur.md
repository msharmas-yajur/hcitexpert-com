<!-- wp:heading {"level":1} -->
<h1>OBJECTIVES</h1>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The utility of telemedicine (TM) in neurosurgery is underexplored, with most of the&nbsp;studies relating to teletrauma or telestroke programs. In this study, the authors evaluate the&nbsp;cost-effectiveness of TM consultations for followup care of a large population of patients who&nbsp;underwent neurosurgical procedures.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":1} -->
<h1>METHODS</h1>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A decision-analytical model was used to assess the cost-effectiveness of TM for&nbsp;elective post–neurosurgical care patients from a predominantly nonurban cohort in West&nbsp;Bengal, India. The model compared TM care via a nodal center in West Bengal to routine, in-person, per-episode care at the provider site in Bangalore, India.&nbsp;<br><br>Cost and effectiveness data&nbsp;relating to 1200 patients were collected for a 52-month period. The effectiveness of TM care&nbsp;was calculated using efficiency in terms of the percentage of successful TM consultations, as&nbsp;well as patient-perceived utility values for overall experience of the type of health care access&nbsp;that they received.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"linkDestination":"custom"} -->

<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><br>Incremental cost-effectiveness ratio (ICER) analysis was done using the 4-quadrant charting of the cost-effectiveness plane. One-way sensitivity and tornado analyses&nbsp;were performed to identify thresholds where the care strategy would change.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":1} -->
<h1>RESULTS</h1>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The overall utility for the 3 TM scenarios was found to be higher (89%) than for the&nbsp;utility of routine care (80%). TM was found to be more cost-effective (Indian rupee [INR] 2630&nbsp;per patient) compared to routine care (INR 6848 per patient).&nbsp;<br><br>The TM strategy “dominates”&nbsp;that of routine care by being more effective and less expensive (ICER value of -39,400 INR/unit&nbsp;of effectiveness). Sensitivity analysis revealed that cost-effectiveness of TM was most sensitive&nbsp;to changes in the number of TM patients, utility and success rate of TM, and travel distance to&nbsp;the TM center.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":1} -->
<h1>CONCLUSIONS</h1>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>TM care dominates the in-person care strategy by providing more effective and&nbsp;less expensive follow-up care for a remote post–neurosurgical care population in India. In the&nbsp;authors’ setting, this benefit of TM is sustainable even if half the TM consultations turn out to&nbsp;be unsuccessful. The viability of TM as a cost-effective care protocol is attributed to a&nbsp;combination of factors, like an adequate patient volume utilizing TM, patient utility, success&nbsp;rate of TM, and the patient travel distance.<br><br><strong><em>Link to Full Article : <a href="https://thejns.org/doi/abs/10.3171/2018.2.FOCUS17543">https://thejns.org/doi/abs/10.3171/2018.2.FOCUS17543</a></em></strong><br><em><strong>Link to VIDEO Presentation by the Authors: </strong><strong><a href="https://vimeo.com/262374146">https://vimeo.com/262374146</a></strong></em><br><strong><em>The Article has been published earlier and is re-published here with the author's permission</em></strong></p>

<h2 id="9e8b">Digital health’s COVID-19 patchy track record can pave way for better solutions in future.</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p id="4617">Since the time the pandemic began, there has&nbsp;been a lot of hype about digital tools and products to help, right from diagnosis through treatment and post-covid management. In each of these fields, the record though is clearly ambivalent when it comes to ultimate impact. Consider these examples-</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li><strong>Contact tracing apps</strong>: As&nbsp;<a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2021/05/27/business/apple-google-virus-tracing-app.html"><strong><em>this report from NYT</em></strong></a>&nbsp;suggests ‘there is little rigorous research to date on whether the apps’ potential to accurately alert people of virus exposures outweighs potential drawbacks — like falsely warning unexposed people, over-testing or failing to detect users exposed to the virus.’</li><li><strong>‘AI’ diagnosis of chest X-Ray</strong>: Those who read my writings know that when I see any ‘AI’ driven product, I take the ‘AI’ piece with a pinch of salt! This&nbsp;<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-021-00338-7"><strong><em>excellent review in Nature Machine Intelligence</em></strong></a>, authors write about ‘a nearly ideal scenario for AI to learn these spurious ‘shortcuts’’ because of the way training data is collected for these algorithms.</li><li>Of the 26 health systems surveyed by MedCity news,&nbsp;<a href="https://medcitynews.com/2021/04/in-scramble-to-respond-to-covid-19-hospitals-turned-to-models-with-high-risk-of-bias/?rf=1"><strong><em>nearly half used automated tools to respond to the Covid-19 pandemic</em></strong></a>, but none of them were regulated.</li></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p id="a365">So what can we learn from this?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li>Developers designing algorithmic decision making systems should pay more attention to the input data quality. The Nature review suggests, especially in the AI-radiology context,&nbsp;<strong>improved collection of training data</strong>&nbsp;that matches the target population and improved choice of the prediction task to&nbsp;<strong>involve more clinically relevant labels</strong>.</li><li>In a brilliant commentary, Topol and Obermeyer recommend considering&nbsp;<strong>patient/user perspectives</strong>&nbsp;in the design. They write- ‘Algorithms based on datasets that pair images or waveforms with “labels” assigned by a doctor have the potential to drive improvements in efficiency and diagnostic accuracy. However, the strength of this approach can also be its weakness: by matching the performance of doctors, algorithms will also incorporate their inherent limitations.’</li><li>Finally, there is a greater need today for regulatory agencies worldwide to start focusing on this field</li></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<h2 id="b73b">Medical device regulations landscape in EU/UK is a veritable alphabet soup!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p id="cb11">On that note, it is interesting to note two important changes in the medical device regulations that would have a direct impact on EU and UK markets but most probably also some wider implications globally.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li>The European Commission’s Medical Device Regulation (MDR) entered into force in May 2017, replacing the Medical Devices Directive. The new regulation is supposed to drive safety profiles of these devices.</li><li>An immediate fallout has been for UK and Switzerland.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=4acbb474-f40b-4543-8168-8394e9f3be47"><strong><em>UK manufactured medical devices</em></strong></a>&nbsp;will need to comply with the MDR when placed on the EU market, the UKCA (if already applicable) has not been recognized as valid equivalent.</li><li><a href="https://www.medtechdive.com/news/swiss-medtech-sounds-alarm-as-mdr-erects-barriers-to-eu-market/600982/"><strong><em>In Switzerland</em></strong></a>, failure to update a Mutual Recognition Agreement ahead of the implementation of the Medical Device Regulation will mean, like post-Brexit UK, Switzerland is now a third country for the purposes of the regulation of medical devices.</li><li><a href="https://medtech.pharmaintelligence.informa.com/MT143902/Fit-For-The-Future-Industry-Gets-Involved-As-UK-Medtech-And-Digital-Health-Care-Regulation-Reaches-Turning-Point"><strong><em>In the UK, industry groups are consulting with MHRA</em></strong></a>&nbsp;and hopeful that the UKCA will evolve more closely with MDR.</li></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p id="7aa0">Unlike the MDD, the MDR imposes for the first time specific obligations on EU importers and distributors and with that makes EU an important market; so MDR will get to set a tone for global regulations governing medical devices.</p>

<h2 id="50a9">Digital Healthcare news-New partners, Confusions and Funding update</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li><a href="https://www.axios.com/amazon-care-big-employers-ee5a8f89-b1da-4cd2-9cc4-e26597afb04d.html"><strong><em>Big Tech</em></strong></a>: Amazon’s new healthcare business Amazon Care, has an enterprise client, Precor, which would use a pay-per-user model i.e. it will charge the fitness equipment manufacturer based on how many employees use its services each month. This is a departure from fixed cost contracts prevalent in this sector.</li><li><a href="https://www.healthcaredive.com/news/kaiser-permanente-mayo-team-up-on-investment-in-acute-care-at-home/600127/"><strong><em>Kaiser Permanente and Mayo Clinic are investing $100 million in a hospital-at-home company</em></strong></a>-Boston-based Medically Home.</li><li>The FDA approved its 50th antibody in 2015, 29 years after the first one.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/d41573-021-00079-7"><strong><em>It took just 6 more years to reach number 100</em></strong></a>, with the approval of GlaxoSmithKline’s PD1 blocker dostarlimab in April.</li><li>Speaking of drug development, clinical trials continue to attract new digitized, siteless models. The latest to enter is&nbsp;<a href="http://pharmacy%20giant%20cvs%20health%20launches%20new%20clinical%20trial%20services%20biz%2C%20eyes%20siteless%20trials%20platform/"><strong><em>CVS Health</em></strong></a>, taking it up from</li><li><a href="https://www.mobihealthnews.com/news/fda-greenlights-bigfoot-biomedicals-insulin-recommending-diabetes-management-system"><strong><em>FDA gave 510(k) clearance for Bigfoot Biomedical</em></strong></a>’s insulin recommending diabetes management system, for patients with Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes who use multiple-dose injection therapy. The system’s smartpen caps take data from the FreeStyle Libre 2 continuous glucose monitor to give users insulin dose recommendations based on a physician’s instructions.</li><li>Pharma-digital health partnerships are changing.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/lilly-inks-smart-insulin-pen-deals-roche-dexcom-and-more"><strong><em>Eli Lilly has signed compatibility agreements with four international providers</em></strong></a>&nbsp;including glucose monitoring sensors, digital health programs and other tools from Roche, Dexcom, Glooko and myDiabby Healthcare.</li><li>Telehealth providers&nbsp;<a href="https://www.mobihealthnews.com/news/amwell-looks-third-party-device-integrations-launch-converge"><strong><em>Amwell, Verizon and Doximity all launched latest virtual care offerings/platforms</em></strong></a>-Amwell’s is called Converge, Verizon’s BlueJeans Telehealth and Doximity’s Dialer.</li><li>On 14th May 2021,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-57111615"><strong><em>Ireland’s Health Service Executive (HSE)</em></strong></a>’s computer systems, became a target for a ransomware attack.</li></ul>

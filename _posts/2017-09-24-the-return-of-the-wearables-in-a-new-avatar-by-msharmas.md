According to the International Data Corporation's (IDC) Worldwide Quarterly Wearable Device Tracker, </span><span><i><b>vendors will ship a total of 125.5 million wearable devices this year, marking a 20.4% increase from the 104.3 million units shipped in 2016</b></i></span><span>. </span><span>From there, </span><span><b><i>the wearables market will nearly double before reaching a total of 240.1 million units shipped in 2021, resulting in a five-year CAGR of 18.2%. </i></b></span><span>[1]</span></div><h1 style="line-height:1.56;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:24pt;text-align:justify;"><span><a name='more'></a><br /></span></h1><h1 style="line-height:1.56;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:24pt;text-align:justify;"><span>The wearables market is entering a new phase</span></h1><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:6pt;text-align:justify;"><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span><span><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">In the first phase of the market development, it was about getting the product out, to generate awareness and interest and getting the customers accustomed to the idea. </span></span></span><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">This opportunity remains to be explored by the traditional and fashion brands as the scale of consumer electronics market evolves</span></span><span>. Now, the wearables market is entering a new phase, opines IDC's Ramon T. Llamas.</span><br /><br /><blockquote class="tr_bq"><span><b><i>Now it’s about getting the experience right – from the way the hardware looks and feels to how software collects, analyzes, and presents insightful data. </i></b><i><b>What this means for users is that in the years ahead, they will be treated to second- and third-generation devices that will make the today’s devices seem quaint. Expect digital assistants, cellular connectivity, and connections to larger systems, both at home and at work. </b>At the same time<b>, expect to see a proliferation in the diversity of devices brought to market, and a decline in prices that will make these more affordable to a larger crowd.” [1]</b></i></span><b><i> </i></b></blockquote><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">The phase 2 of the wearables development appears to be about taking the user data and provide analytics around the data to provide insights to the user, like step counts translate into a healthier heart. In this phase its about getting the customer to see the devices that actually augment the abilities to make lives easier, healthier and more productive, rather than another screen for the user. The report however shows that the basic watches (devices that do not run third party applications, including hybrid watches, fitness/GPS watches, and most kid watches) will continue out-ship smart watches (devices capable of running third party applications, like Apple Watch, Samsung Gear, and all Android Wear devices), as numerous <b><i>traditional watch makers shift more resources to building hybrid watches</i></b>, creating a greater TAM each year. The report suggests that the <b><i>Smart watches, however, will see a boost in volumes in 2019</i></b> as cellular connectivity on the watches becomes more prevalent on the market.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><b>Wrist Bands:</b> The report indicates a slow down in the market for the wristbands from 2016 onwards, but the market will be propped up with low-cost devices with good enough features for the mass market. However, the trend seems to focus on the users transitioning to watches for additional utility and multi-purpose use.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><b>Earwear: (</b>this excludes the bluetooth headsets) are not counting. Examples include Bragi’s Dash and Samsung Gear Icon X. The report, also suggests the increase in the uptake of smarter earwear that centers on collecting fitness data about the user, real-time audio filtering or language translation.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><b>Clothing:</b>  The smart clothing market took a strong step forward driven by the chines vendors providing <b>connected apparel</b>. For instance, the upcoming release of Google and Levi’s Project Jacquared-enabled jacket.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><b>Others:</b> include lesser known products like clip-on devices, non-AR/VR eyewear, and others into this category.

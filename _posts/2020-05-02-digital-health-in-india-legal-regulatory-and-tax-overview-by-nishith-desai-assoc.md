<p class="has-drop-cap">The current legal and regulatory landscape that governs Digital Health is scattered and ambiguous. To make matters worse, there is none or very little legal scholarship in the area of&nbsp;Digital Health in India. The scope of Digital Health is vast and covers various business models, which inherently makes it difficult to regulate as a whole. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This paper seeks to&nbsp;<strong><em>knit together existing laws and regulations</em></strong> into what may be called an <strong><em>“ad-hoc” legal framework for&nbsp;Digital Health in India</em></strong>. It is being written <strong><em>for those&nbsp;who are already invested in Digital Health as workforce or capital contributors</em></strong> as well as those who are still testing the waters. Since this is <strong><em>a research paper</em></strong>, it also seeks <strong><em>to raise questions and takes positions</em></strong> which are yet to be tested with the hope that it&nbsp;would set the tone <strong><em>for&nbsp;legal discussions in larger platforms</em></strong> </p>

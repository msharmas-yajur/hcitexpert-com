We finally focus on Product as it relates to the "why" and "what" work needs to be done.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Traditionally most people talk about products focusing on Feature - Function - Benefit methodology which has worked well for the past 40 years since it was introduced by IBM in 1976. With changes in times as well as proliferation of products, to differentiate their products, people also started to focus on USP (Unique Selling Proposition), RoI (Return on Investment), Efficiency savings, increased Effectiveness,  reduced TC (Total Cost of Ownership) etc. These are logical things which appeal to the mind, however many times we still are not able to decide on a specific product given this multitude of facts and figures.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"></span></span><br /><a name='more'></a><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">This is where the Golden Circle from <a href="https://www.startwithwhy.com/About.aspx" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Simon Sinek</a> comes in handy.  Focus is on moving from the Why to the What. It helps us understand the right way to reach our potential customers/partners and drives to deeper meaning on the very existence of the product.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span><br /><div class="separator" style="clear:both;text-align:center;"><a href="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/45549-goldencircle.jpg" style="margin-left:1em;margin-right:1em;"><img border="0" height="283" src="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/e4db3-goldencircle.jpg" width="400" /></a></div><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">The focus is now no longer just on the functionality that the product offers or how it achieves certain business objectives.

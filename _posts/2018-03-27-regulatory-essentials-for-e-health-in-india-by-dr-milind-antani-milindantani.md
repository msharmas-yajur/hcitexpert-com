style="font-size:18.6667px;">A doctor should not give any advice over electronic media that would ordinarily require the physical examination </span></span><span style="color:#695d46;font-size:18.6667px;">of the patient.</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» The Supreme Court has noted that prescriptions should generally not be given out without actual examination.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» It has also stated that prescriptions should not be given over the telephone, except in case of emergency.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"></span></span><br /><a name='more'></a><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Ensure that your doctors/healthcare service providers are duly registered with the relevant state medical councils.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» Keep in mind that certain states require the doctor to be registered in the relevant state where the advice is </span></span><span>being provided/patient is situated.</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Obtain informed consent from the patient before providing advice over telemedicine. Consent should have </span></span><span>declarations that patient:</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» has attained the legal age of majority;</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» is voluntarily providing personal and medical information;</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» has read the privacy policy, terms of use and other documentation;</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» consents to the provider intimating public authorities about results and findings during the course of services, </span></span><span style="color:#695d46;font-size:18.6667px;">if required by law;</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» understands the inherent risks related to the provision of telemedicine and other related services;</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» is aware that s/he may withdraw consent at any time; and</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» can inspect and modify personal information provided at any time.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Make sure that the patient has read about the inherent limitations of telemedicine that arise due to absence </span></span><span>of physical contact between the doctor and the patient.</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» Ensure that the patient is aware that the issuance of a prescription is not guaranteed.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Make it clear to the patient that telemedicine services are not for emergencies.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» Build in disclaimers that state that telemedicine services should not be used in case of an emergency.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» However, in case of one, please do not shy away from providing whatever assistance that you can.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Ensure that no one other than the doctor is privy to the consultation, as it may result in breach of the patient’s </span></span><span>privacy. </span><span>Have a privacy policy in place. It should lay down:</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» whose personal information is being collected;</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» for what purpose;</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» until when; and</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» whether it will be disclosed/transferred to a third party or not.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Have a terms of use of service in place and clearly identify:</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» limitation of liability;</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» indemnity; and</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» the jurisdiction of courts.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Bear in mind that e-prescriptions require digital signature of the doctor.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» A prescription carrying a picture of the doctor’s signature may not be a valid prescription.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» A scanned copy of a physical prescription may also not be considered valid.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"> Maintain records of the consultation to the extent possible.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» The period of limitation for civil cases is 3 years. Maintaining records for this period at the minimum would help </span></span><span>mitigate risk.</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» The government is contemplating making it mandatory to maintain records of OPD patients.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Always request patients to share contact information of a person who may be reached in case of an emergency </span></span><span>during consultation. </span><span>There are inherent limitations of operating a platform model versus a service model.</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» In the platform model, the service provider cannot monitor quality beyond a point, else it will lose the status </span></span><span>of a platform provider.</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» In the service model, the quality can be monitored to a great extent. </span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">However, there is a risk of litigation against </span></span><span>the service provider for any deficiency in service rendered by the doctor.</span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">Documentation is key! Make sure you have all the required documents in place to mitigate risk.</span></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">» Proper documentation will help in clearly demarcating roles and responsibilities, which becomes essential </span></span><span>in ascertaining liability.</span><br /><span><br /></span> <b><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">What are the changes you invisage in the legal framework governing Telemedicine services in india?</span></span><span> </span></b><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><br /></span></span> <span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;"><b>Dr. Milind Antani:</b> I would consider e-Health more relevant than Telemedicine as e-Health has broader scope of activities. India has been witnessing significant upward surge in e-Health recently.

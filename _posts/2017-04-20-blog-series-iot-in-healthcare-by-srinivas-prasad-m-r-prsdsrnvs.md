Presenting the insights shared by </span><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad </span><span>(<a href="https://twitter.com/prsdsrnvs" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">@prsdsrnvs</a>) on </span><span><b><i>#IoT in Healthcare #PhilipsChat</i></b></span><span>. (</span><span><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M._R._Srinivasaprasad" style="text-decoration-line:none;">http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M._R._Srinivasaprasad</a>)</span></div><span><b style="font-weight:normal;"></b></span><br /><a name='more'></a><span><b style="font-weight:normal;"><br /></b></span> <br /><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Q1: In the near term (1-3 years), What are the top 3 innovations in IoT that can benefit healthcare?: </span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span><span>In the emerging markets the IOT devices that would play an important role in extending care to the homes and community, would be those that can enable remote monitoring of cardiac patients, COPD patients and pregnancy monitoring in the rural community. These devices could be devices like Connect diagnostic ECG, Low cost but reliable wearables to monitor basic vital signs and breathing patterns and connected intelligent fetal dopplers to help monitor the child during birth helped by midwives </span></div><br /><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Q2: Is an IoT based system going to be a utility or a service?: </span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span><span>This will be a mix of both. It can (also) be an Outcome-based pricing model which is a variant implementation of the Service Model.</span></div><br /><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Q3. Do you see any device, connected via any protocol and with any cloud; as the future, if yes how will that be achieved? Standards?: </span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span><span>Need to adhere to Continua standard for these devices for open connectivity but in the near term I see that cost will prevail over the interoperability standard if regulatory bodies don't ensure conformance from the start.</span><span> </span><br /><span><br /></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Q4: In India (or your country), what are the Digital Infrastructure requirements for enabling IoT based Innovations in Healthcare?: </span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span><span>A change in the mindset of going from paper to paperless is needed. Then there is a need to educate hospitals “Software is not free”. In addition, enabling IoT will need reliable telecom networks, work with the ecosystem to set up datacenters. Maturity &amp; innovation around commercial business models will be a need Supporting infrastructure like connected ambulances, trained paramedics &amp; and efficient transportation system will help.</span><br /><span><br /></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Q5. How can hospitals leverage #IoT based solutions for service delivery and patient care? : </span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span><span>Postoperative care can be shifted to the patient's home. This can help free up beds in the hospital which can help in increase revenue to the hospital from a new patient. Hospital-acquired infections can be reduced and finally, in bringing down the cost of care, the benefit that can be extended to the patient. </span><br /><span><br /></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Q6: What are the aspects of Connected Care for the Patient Care Continuum? </span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span><span>From Philips Healthcare point of view Connected care for the Patient Care Continuum comprises of Healthy Living, Prevention, Diagnosis, Treatment and Homecare. Here’s a video that explains these aspects </span><span><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xe-KxiiIyNI">https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xe-KxiiIyNI</a></span></div><br /><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Q7. Please share usecases for Connected Care for: Healthy Living, Prevention, Diagnosis, Treatment, Homecare: </span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span><span>(elaborated on each of the aspects of Connected Care for the Patient Care Continuum)</span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>#HealthyLiving</span><span> There are many in the market but it is important to choose the right one like the ones which are accurate e.g. Philips watch. Eating healthy food but tasty from an Indian cuisine context means fried food. Philips air fryer helps air fry tasty healthy food. </span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span>#Prevention</span><span> Breathing Fresh &amp; clean air is important for us to avoid pulmonary complications in countries like ours where pollution levels are high. #Philips Air Purifier helps address this issue</span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span>#Diagnosis</span><span> </span><span>Early diagnosis is critical to increase survival rate and reduce the cost of treatment. Use of AI in helping clinicians early diagnosis and also managing a larger  population base can help solve the India challenges. Either for aiding in detection of infectious diseases like TB or identifying lesions from brain and breast scans.</span><br /><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span>#Treatment</span><span> Minimally invasive devices are the key here . Low dose interventional X-ray systems or mobile surgery systems . Radiation planning systems which aid accurate and the right dose planning.</span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span>#HomeCare</span><span> Monitoring of post cardiac patients and COPD patients at home. Philips Home Co business in india extends care into the home. This reduces cost of care and also helps the hospital to manage more new patients. Monitoring compliance to medication is another key aspect when extending care to home.</span><br /><span><br /></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Q8: What are the Healthcare based Smart City components? How can Local, State and National Government’s make #IoT solutions in healthcare economically viable?: </span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span><span>Government should step in with policies that support in “giving the last mile connectivity” to decentralize healthcare (and) help in the convergence of Mobile technology, Consumer engagement and Payment reforms.</span><br /><span>Additionally, Population management to understand disease profiling, to understand the spread of infectious diseases like malaria, typhoid etc. Smart ambulances. Garbage clearing monitoring. Air quality monitoring device across the city. Adequate availability of AED 's and accessible and connected. These are all related to health care.</span><br /><span><br /></span><span><b><i>Q9. How can private hospitals justify the RoI’s of Smart Hospital Components?</i></b></span><br /><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;font-weight:bold;">M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span>Demanding solutions from healthcare companies. Looking at OPEx models where the capex requirement is low. Productivity improvement from workflow efficiencies. Productivity gains or clinicians from using AI and smart tools. Better clinical decisions and patient re-admissions which will be enhance patient stickiness and also enhance the brand which will drive more patients to the hospital.</span><br /><br /><span><b><i>Q10. Tell us a 5 Year view of IoT in Healthcare and what would a Patient Experience in a Smart Hospital?</i></b></span><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;font-weight:bold;">M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span>A seamless experience which helps the hospital in its business, helps the clinicians make better decisions, helps patients by reducing their healthcare costs and the insurance providers become more efficient and manage their premiums better. A win win for all.  Example from the onset of chest pain to early diagnosis, to emergency care, seamless patient data flow into the hospital EMR's. Flow or patient context and information right through the various departments in the hospital including radiology, cardiology, critical care and also seamless extension of this care into home or the community post discharge and post operative care on remote care settings. A seamless experience in a distributed care environment</span><br /><br /><span><b><i>Q11. What areas of IoT based innovations are you looking to partner with Startups for? Can you give us two areas? </i></b></span><span><span style="font-size:18.6667px;font-weight:bold;">M. R. Srinivas Prasad: </span>Advanced AI models to aid early diagnosis of chronic diseases - Cardiac, COPD, Oncology and Mother and Child space. Smart IOT  devices to support Monitoring of patients under various clinical conditions post discharge. Pregnancy monitoring and post natal care. Solutions in the healthcare informatics space and connect care solutions to help extend care into the community and homes </span><br /><span><br /></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>To one other question from </span><span>Mr. NS Ramnath (<a href="https://twitter.com/rmnth" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">@rmnth</a>)</span><span>: </span><span><b><i>On a scale of 1 to 10, where do you think the sensors in wearables in the market today stand?</i></b></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;text-align:justify;"><span>M.

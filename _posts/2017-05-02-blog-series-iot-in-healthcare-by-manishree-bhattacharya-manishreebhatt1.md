How are we embracing this change? The <a href="http://blog.hcitexpert.com/2016/05/infographic-iot-in-healthcare-opportunities.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Types of Opportunities</a></span><span> that present themselves to the Startups, Healthcare IT organisations are tremendous. </span><br /><span></span> <br /><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>During the #PhilipsChat, on the 10th April 2017, we asked the experts what they thought about the current trends and focus areas that the IT Industry, Medical Device Manufacturers, Hospitals and Start-ups will need to keep in view, in the near and short-term, while making their organisation ready for the Digital Transformation that can be and will be enabled by #IoT in Healthcare.  </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Presenting the insights shared by </span><span>Manishree Bhattacharya</span><span> (@ManishreeBhatt1) on #IoT in Healthcare</span></span><br /><span></span><br /><a name='more'></a><span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Q1: In the near term (1-3 years), What are the top 3 innovations in IoT that can benefit healthcare?</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Manishree Bhattacharya: </span><span>1. Remote monitoring of (cardiac disorders, COPD, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, insomnia, diabetes, elderly, expecting mothers)</span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span>2. An integrated/connected surgical room, where devices are interoperable, regularly feeds in data into patient profile in EMR, to streamline post-operative care, both in the hospital and beyond, at patient homes</span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span>3. IoT for ensuring drug/treatment adherence, such as sensor-based pills</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:24pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Q2. Do you see any device, connected via any protocol and with any cloud; as the future, if yes how will that be achieved? Standards? </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Manishree Bhattacharya: </span><span>Right now, developments are quite random and sporadic. To achieve larger goals, moving from connected devices to connected hospitals, some level of standardization and uniformity will be important to ensure an error-free, and secured transmission. </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:25pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Q3: In India (or your country), what are the Digital Infrastructure requirements for enabling IoT based Innovations in Healthcare? </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Manishree Bhattacharya: </span><span>Seeing Digital Health take off in India in its full bloom is one of my wishes, and the preliminary requisite would be to encourage hospitals go paper-less - have EHR systems implemented, with a timeline set for nation-wide implementation. Just imagine how seamless healthcare delivery will be if primary, secondary and tertiary centres are integrated – data can seamlessly flow from one centre to another. Government has a very strong role to play here, that will help in creating the right infrastructure, timely adoption, establishing standards, lowering costs by promoting local manufacturing, and boosting HealthIT start-ups.</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:24pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Q4. Please share use cases for Connected Care for: Healthy Living, Prevention, Diagnosis, Treatment, Homecare: </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Manishree Bhattacharya: </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Healthy Living </span><span>– Most consumer IoT devices aim to do that – tracking exercise regimes, diet plans</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Prevention</span><span> - Say a heart patient puts on a wearable device that continuously monitors and sends signals to nurses/doctors for any aberration - this can ensure timely treatment and prevent a severe episode.</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Homecare</span><span> - A person who has just had a surgery, and is on homecare - his regular vitals, diet plan, outputs are remotely being tracked by the doctor/nurse - who can selectively revise the diet or post-surgery recovery plan. Same goes with elderly who are on home-care.</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Treatment</span><span> - A sensor-based pill that sends a signal to a care-giver on ingestion of the pill.</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>The bigger purpose - We know that not all medicines work on every patient. Regularly tracking patients not only help in timely interventions, and more personalized treatments, it also opens routes to more clinical research on </span><span>personalized medicines</span><span>. </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:24pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Q5: What are the Healthcare based Smart City components? How can Local, State and National Government’s make #IoT solutions in healthcare economically viable? </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Manishree Bhattacharya: </span><span>Answering to how can government make IoT solutions viable, my thoughts would be:</span></span></div><ol style="margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;"><li><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>By promoting indigenous manufacturing to curb costs</span></span></div></li><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><li><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;"><span>Incentivising IoT adoption in hospitals</span></div></li><li><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:0;"><span style="font-size:14pt;vertical-align:baseline;white-space:pre-wrap;">Prioritizing HealthIT in the overall start-up agenda</span></div></li></span></ol><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:24pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Q6: How can private hospitals justify the RoI’s of Smart Hospital Components?</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Manishree Bhattacharya: </span><span>By improving quality of care; reducing hospital re-admissions, yet prolonging the care process that extends to one's home; and finally improving patient engagement/adherence. A patient is more likely to visit a doctor who can provide a more personalized treatment than the one who cannot. Important would be define these key metrics/KPIs right at the beginning of implementation.</span></span><br /><span><br /></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Q7. Tell us a 5 Year view of IoT in Healthcare and what would a Patient Experience be in a Smart Hospital? </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Manishree Bhattacharya: </span><span>First, we have to understand the purpose of IoT in healthcare - it is not there just for the sake of it, but to truly enable a coordinated and long-term care, that would eventually reduce mortality, morbidity, and hospital re-admissions. Patient experience is bound to improve. A patient will not have to run from one department to another, narrating the whole problem and showing multiple reports. So when a cancer in-patient enters a psychologist's office, and the doctor already knows the problem, and also has the latest vitals of the patient right in his tablet, he knows that the patient was not able to get any sleep the previous night and has a high BP right now. The doctor would hence probably choose to talk about things that can ease the patient's current situation. Now, that is truly an enriching experience. </span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span><br class="kix-line-break" /></span><span>Looking ahead in the future, we may also have AI-enabled voice assistants that will make a patient more comfortable in hospital settings.</span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:24pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Q8. Finally: What areas of IoT based innovations are you looking to partner with Startups for? Can you give us two areas? </span></span></div><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-650cd66a-997f-0192-8ab7-fb947796921a"><span>Manishree Bhattacharya: </span><span>Would love to connect with any start-up that can provide meaningful solutions for the Indian healthcare landscape. What I would also like to see is how these start-ups are using the tonnes of data that IoT devices generate, in deriving meaningful analysis – big data, AI, and so on.</span></span></div><h1

A 40% Y/Y growth and 29% penetration. This presents a large customer base for the Digital Health Startups providing services online</span></div></li><li style="background-color:transparent;color:#695d46;font-family:'PT Serif';font-size:18.666666666667px;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;font-weight:400;list-style-type:decimal;text-decoration:none;vertical-align:baseline;"><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;text-align:justify;"><span>India is number 1 in terms of the number of Android apps downloaded, greater than the US. So an obvious first choice of platform on mobiles</span></div></li><li style="background-color:transparent;color:#695d46;font-family:'PT Serif';font-size:18.666666666667px;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;font-weight:400;list-style-type:decimal;text-decoration:none;vertical-align:baseline;"><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;text-align:justify;"><span>India has been recording a steady growth in the smartphone shipments, at 15% Y/Y</span></div></li><li style="background-color:transparent;color:#695d46;font-family:'PT Serif';font-size:18.666666666667px;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;font-weight:400;list-style-type:decimal;text-decoration:none;vertical-align:baseline;"><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;text-align:justify;"><span>There has been a reduction in the cost of 1GB of data from $3.15/ 1GB to about $2/ 1GB. Including Jio, the cost comes down to $0.33/ 1GB</span></div></li><li style="background-color:transparent;color:#695d46;font-family:'PT Serif';font-size:18.666666666667px;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;font-weight:400;list-style-type:decimal;text-decoration:none;vertical-align:baseline;"><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:0;text-align:justify;"><span>There has been a push from the Government towards "Digital" services as can be seen from the following initiatives: Jan Dhan, Digital India, Skill India, Startup India. Government’s Policies rolled out with speed and scope</span></div></li><li style="background-color:transparent;color:#695d46;font-family:'PT Serif';font-size:18.666666666667px;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;font-weight:400;list-style-type:decimal;text-decoration:none;vertical-align:baseline;"><span>India identity via Aadhar + eKYC, Digital authentication of 1B+ people, 82% of the population have aadhar, has the potential to enable services with speed, scale and scope (e.g., SIM card activation from 1-3 days to 15 minutes)</span></li><li style="background-color:transparent;color:#695d46;font-family:'PT Serif';font-size:18.666666666667px;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;font-weight:400;list-style-type:decimal;text-decoration:none;vertical-align:baseline;"><span>46% of Internet users primarily consume local language content. 6 Languages spoken by &gt; 50MM users (excluding english)</span></li><li style="background-color:transparent;color:#695d46;font-family:'PT Serif';font-size:18.666666666667px;font-style:normal;font-variant:normal;font-weight:400;list-style-type:decimal;text-decoration:none;vertical-align:baseline;"><span>Young India: 64 % of the population, 72% of the Internet users less than 35 years of age</span></li><li style="background-color:transparent;color:#695d46;font-family:'PT India Healthcare Story</span></h1><div><ol style="margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;"><li><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">India Healthcare has a high and rising out of pocket spend, &lt;20% insurance penetration (a potential area for disruption?)</span></li><li><span style="font-size:18.6667px;">India Healthcare reimagined: Increasingly accessible via mobile and affordable via online aggregation and price transparency. Savings on Services like Online pharmacy (20-30%), lab tests ordering (40-50%).</span></li></ol></div><h1 style="line-height:1.56;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:24pt;"><span>The Digital Health at an Inflection Point</span></h1><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:6pt;"><span>There are some interesting insights related to the Digital Health being at an Inflection point. </span><span>These are:</span></div><ol style="margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;"><li><div style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:6pt;"><span>100 years ago: human touch; 25 years ago: machine assisted/ analog; Today: technology enabled/ digital</span></div></li><li><div style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;"><span>4 trends highlight the current Digitisation of Healthcare and the Virtuous Cycle of Innovation: <i style="font-weight:bold;">Data Inputs, Data Accumulation, Data Insights, Translation. </i>Together these trends are helping measure Outcomes and rapidly iterate to enable compression of Innovation Cycle times.</span></div></li><li><div style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;"><span>The earlier analog medical technology is increasingly being replaced with Digital Technology, and is (continuously being) connected</span></div></li><li><div style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:0;"><span>Diagnostics Technology: increase in the number of measured / monitored data attributes</span></div></li><li><span>Increasing adoption in the use of wearables, for health and wellness. For health, heart rate and temperature</span></li><li><span>Leading technology brands are well positioned to participate in the Digital Health wave, with the customers stating in a Rock Health survey they will be most willing to share healthcare data with the likes of Google, Microsoft, Samsung, Apple, Amazon, Facebook and IBM (in that order)</span></li><li><span>Data Accumulation enabling the proliferation of Digitally native healthcare related datasets</span></li><li><span>Proliferation of health apps, with a 5% Y/Y growth in the US and 15% Y/Y growth in rest of the world</span></li><li><span>EHR adoption is leading to a broad and centralised accumulation of data with various types of patient data elements in a native digital dataset , e.g., clinical results, scanned images, vital signs, problems, medications, allergies, etc</span></li><li><span>In the US there has been an increase in the number of hospitals allowing for the patients digital access to the healthcare information</span></li><li><span>There has been an increasing digitisation of inputs fueling a 48% Y/Y growth in healthcare data</span></li><li style="list-style-type:decimal;vertical-align:baseline;"><span>Data Accumulation: a typical 500-bed hospital generates 50 petabytes of data (1 petabyte = 1 mm gigabytes).</span></li><li><span>Rise in inputs + increase in digital healthcare data = medical research and knowledge is doubling every 3.5 years</span></li></ol><div dir="ltr" id="docs-internal-guid-7f8576b5-ff00-784f-b097-8486d6015797" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:10pt;"><span>Now that you have these data points available to you, what are the Digital Health business categories can you think of?

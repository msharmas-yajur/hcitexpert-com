<div dir="ltr" style="text-align:left;"><b>What is Connected Care?</b><br /><span><br /></span> <span>Alliance for Connected Care (http://www.connectwithcare.org/what-is-connected-care/) defines Connected Care as: </span><br /><div dir="ltr" style="margin-bottom:0;margin-top:6pt;text-align:justify;"><div style="line-height:2.0736;"><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:6pt;"><blockquote class="tr_bq"><a href="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/7d465-connected-health-infographic-1.png" style="clear:left;float:left;margin-bottom:1em;margin-right:1em;"></a><span><i><b>Connected Care is</b> the <b>real-time</b>, <b>electronic communication</b> <b>between</b> a <b>patient</b> and a <b>provider</b>, <b>including</b> <b>telehealth</b>, <b>remote patient monitoring</b>, and <b>secure email communication</b> between clinicians and their patients. Connected Care <b>improves</b> <b>access to care</b>, <b>helps</b> providers and patients <b>avoid costly health care services</b>, and <b>increases convenience for patients</b>.</i></span>  </blockquote><blockquote class="tr_bq"><a href="https://hcitexpert.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/7d465-connected-health-infographic-1.png" style="clear:left;float:left;margin-bottom:1em;margin-right:1em;"></a><i style="color:#695d46;font-size:18.6667px;">Despite the rapidly developing technology and increasing interest among patients and physicians in the use of Connected Care, legal and regulatory barriers continue to limit mainstream acceptance of this technology.

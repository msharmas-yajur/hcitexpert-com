Of India and Mr. Rajesh Batra, CIO &amp; VP, Kokilaben Dhirubhai Ambani Hospital, Mumbai.</span><br /><br /><span>After the keynotes came the panel discussions, both moderated by Dr. Vikram Venkateswaran, Healthcare India. </span><br /><h1 style="line-height:1.56;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:24pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Panel 1: Leveraging Digital India for Healthcare </span></h1><br /><span><b>Mr. Pramod Varma, Chief Architect, Aadhar</b></span><br /><ul><li><span>Frameworks over Aadhar are currently carrying out 50 million + ID authentications a day</span></li><li><span>Aadhar is the basic Identity management framework. Over which additional aspects of a system can be developed.</span></li><li><span>Recently launched the Digital Signature, eSign service, which is currently enabling 2million auth/ day</span></li><li><span>The Digital Locker facility currently being developed over Aadhar framework will allow for creation of EHRs in a federated model. Once the e-sign has been enabled by the patient at a particular location, the Federated model will pull in the patient records from wherever they are currently being stored. There will be no need to extract and move the patient records from their original storage location, instead they will be pulled in on-demand.</span></li><li><span>This will enable paperless storing of patient data &amp; allow for an electronic consent framework</span></li><li><span>UPI will allow for a seamless payments gateway</span></li><li><span>1 Billion people can be authenticated currently, using the Aadhar ID Management system. Jio in India is currently enabling service delivery to the customers using the Aadhar ID management and KYC systems to issue mobile services to customers. It takes just 5 min to issue the SIM card to a new customer.</span></li><li><span>Pramod Varma was of the opinion that IoT hacks happen because of inability to secure the software stack. In most hardware IoT based startups, Hardware aspects are taken up and innovated upon, but network level software security is generally the issue.</span></li><li><span>Software is taking over the architecture at every level, each level needs to be secured</span></li><li><span>Very strong tech exists to control trusted systems </span></li><li><span>In Aadhar, data is digitally signed and encrypted using HW security module at network security level</span></li><li><span>Software security architectures ensures, that the data is also encrypted and stored in the Database. Not even the database administrator is able to see the data</span></li><li><span>It costs Rs. 40 per Citizen enrolled in aadhar</span></li><li><span>The cost of equipment for enrolling Citizens has been bought down to $1500 from $15000</span></li><li><span>Now work is being done on a API based Healthcare platform, details to be published soon </span></li></ul><b><span> <b> </b></span></b><br /><b><span><b>Dr. Pinak Shrikhande, Director, Critinext and Healthquad</b>: </span></b><br /><ul><li><span>To implement solutions in Healthcare, the following three factors need to be considered: Connectivity, Acceptability and Cost of Implementation of services such as Telemed, TeleICUs</span></li><li><span>Additionally, the solutions should also enable productivity and scaling of services</span></li><li><span>Security of Healthcare Data a major concern in the future. How the owner/ generator/ custodian of data will use the data</span><span> needs to be defined<span style="color:black;"><span style="font-family:serif;"><br /></span></span></span></li><li><span>What part of data can be monetised and privacy enabled, will be decided by the individual</span></li></ul><br /><b><span><b>Ravi Gururaj, Head of Product Council, NASSCOM</b>: </span></b><br /><ul><li><span>Chasm in Healthcare needs to be bridged, this certainly cannot be filled by the current technology that is being in most of the Big hospitals</span></li><li><span>Gaps need to be identified and filled by the startups</span></li><li><span>Technology needs to take a frugal shape, it should be self contained, either powered by battery or solar, needs to be scalable and affordable, this should be the focus of the startups</span></li><li><span>For instance, consider the case of CAT scan, the machine needs to be installed at a Capex cost, this should for instance be replaced by a solution that contains Cloud + Sensors + intelligence component. Only this component is taken to the rural setting, reading of results is done remotely</span></li><li><span>Ravi mentioned, Data Aggregation needs to be done at scale, so that insights from treatments for similar patients will be presented for each patient condition.</span></li><li><span>Ravi, opined, security is over hyped and should not be treated as the end of the world. Data Aggregation requires transparency of data, else it stops the discovery of innovations </span></li></ul><br /><span><b>Dilip Kumar, Praxify</b>: </span><br /><ul><li><span>needs of urban hospitals very different from rural hospitals</span></li><li><span>startups prime responsibility to innovate</span></li><li><span>data aggregation/ analytics need to scale at a national level </span></li></ul></div><h1 style="line-height:1.56;margin-bottom:0;margin-top:24pt;text-align:justify;"><span>Panel 2: IoT &amp; AI - A Potent combination defining healthcare</span></h1><div dir="ltr" style="line-height:1.8;margin-bottom:10pt;margin-top:6pt;text-align:justify;"><br /><b><span><b>Dr. Anoop Amarnath, Head Medical Strategy, Manipal Health Enterprises Pvt.
